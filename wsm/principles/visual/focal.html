Law of focal point

On this page

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Take aways

Overview

Definition: Whatever stands out visually (a point of interest, emphasis, or difference) is first to capture and hold the viewer's attention.

In practice: It is the first point of interest that grabs the user. The user then moves to other parts of the page. With the focal point, determine where it is, how many there are, and does it emphasize the correct thing.

Examples

Correct use

  • On the page below, the alert stands out the most, compared to the rest of the content. This is the correct use of a focal point.
  • In the next example, the table of contents is presented first, so the user immediately has a sense of what is on the page.

Incorrect use

  • In the following page, all content elements are given the same design and visual weight, resulting in lack of focus, prioritization, and flow on the page.
  • On the next page, the focus is on a blue "Overview" button, but it is just a repeat of the title "Canada Pension Plan – Overview" and redundantly links users back to this same page.
