Law of past experience

On this page

  • Overview
  • Examples
  • Take aways

Overview

Definition: Objects will be grouped together if the user previously experienced them in that manner.

In practice: Using consistent and repeatable design patterns ensures the user experience is optimized. New designs are at risk of being incorrectly perceived by the user.

Examples

Correct use

  • Ads or "pushed" content is always grouped together. As shown in the example below.

Incorrect use

  • The chevron represents expand/hide and the user expects content to toggle when clicked. In this case, the chevron is just a decoration beside regular links, which is an incorrect use of the law of past experience.
  • Below the title on a content page, any local navigation should be to the content on that page. However, on the next page, the links take the user to related pages in the process.
