Sections

Visual hierarchy
Arrange or present elements in a way that implies importance
Law of proximity
Users perceive objects or shapes that are close together or further apart
Law of similarity
Users perceive items with the same characteristics (similar elements) as related, even if this is not the intention
Law of closure
Users already close off blocks of content without the need to distinctly mark the perimeter
Law of symmetry
User will see a something as connected when there are two symmetrical elements
Law of common fate
Users perceive objects as lines that move along a path and then mentally group them together when they have the same direction of motion
Law of continuity
Users perceive a line as an object that continues along the smoothest path
Law of figure and ground
Users perceive objects as either figures (distinct elements of focus) or ground (background, foreground or landscape on which the figures rest)
Law of focal point
Users will first focus on whatever stands out visually (a point of interest, emphasis, or difference)
Law of past experience
Users will group objects together if they previously experienced them in that manner
