Why the CRA may contact you

There are times when the CRA will need to contact you. They may need to ask for additional or missing information, to discuss an amount you owe, or notify about an upcoming review.

What to expect when the CRA calls

When the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) calls you, the employee will identify themselves. Always be cautious when you receive a phone call from an unknown caller.

Verify first before you call back

Never call the phone number that was given to you, or that shows up on your caller ID unless you have verified it yourself with the CRA.

An employee from CRA will have their own number, but will always allow you to call one of the public numbers to confirm their identity.

Confirm with a correspondence letter

The caller may have first sent you an official CRA letter in regards to what they are calling about. If their name and phone number match what is on the letter, you can also validate the caller this way.

If you’re still unsure the CRA contacted you

If the caller refuses to provide a valid call back number, and there is no further documentation that was mailed to you, this may be a scam.
