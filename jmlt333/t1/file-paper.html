Filing a personal income tax return by paper

You may be looking for:

  • How to file a personal income tax return electronically

Steps

  1. Get the paperwork

    Get the package needed to file your federal and provincial or territorial personal income tax return.

    Refer to: Get a T1 income tax package

    Filing someone else's tax return

    If you are filing a personal income tax return on behalf of someone else, you must be authorized as their representative.

    For more information, refer to: Authorize a representative

  2. Complete the tax return

    1. Enter personal information

      Enter the current personal information, such as name, address, and SIN.

    2. Report income

      What needs to be reported as income, including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, or other earnings.

      If you need a copy of tax slips

      ----EDIT---With NETFILE, you don't need to send your tax information slips, receipts, or other documents used to prepare your tax return unless the CRA asks to see them.

      If you are filing by paper and are missing tax slips, refer to: [Tax slips]

      It is your responsibility to contact the issuer for any missing or incorrect tax slips.

    3. Claim deductions, credits, and expenses

      Find and claim tax deductions, credits, and expenses to reduce the amount of tax owed at the end of the tax year.

    4. Review the information on the tax return

      Before sending the tax return to the CRA, it is your responsibility to review the information to make sure it is accurate.

  3. Send the paper tax return to the CRA

    If there are multiple returns being filed

    If there are multiple returns being filed:

    • Place each person's return in a separate envelope
    • Place the same person's returns all in one envelope
    Transferring amounts to an instalment account

    If you expect a refund and want to have it transferred to an instalment account, you must attach a note to your paper return.

    The CRA will transfer the full amount of your refund directly to your instalment account. It will go toward any amount owing on your tax return. The CRA will consider this payment received on the date that your return is assessed.

    For more information, refer to: Required tax instalments for individuals

    • Mailing to the CRA

      1. Find out where to mail a paper return
      2. Mail your completed tax return to your tax centre

    • Dropping off at a CRA office

      1. Find out the location of CRA's drop boxes
      2. Place your return in a CRA drop box

  4. Proceed with finishing your taxes

    There are 3 possible outcomes from your tax return:

    • Get a refund
    • Have a balance owing
    • Have no refund or balance owing

    You will receive your notice of assessment (NOA) after your tax return is processed. It will indicate the outcome from your tax return.

    If you need to make changes to your tax return, you must wait until you receive your NOA.

    Processing time: Within 8 weeks

    For details, refer to: After filing a tax return
