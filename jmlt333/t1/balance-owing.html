Paying your balance owing

If you have a balance owing, you have options to pay the full amount, or options to pay over time.

  • April 30, 2024 : Deadline to pay your 2023 taxes

Balance owing on your tax return

If you have an amount on line 48500 of your personal income tax return, you have a balance owing.

You should pay any amounts you owe to cover your balance owing by the due date to avoid paying interest and penalties.

For more information, refer to: Interest on taxes you owe

Pay the amount you owe

If your balance owing is $2 or less, you do not have to make a payment.

Do not mail cash or include cash with your return.

If your balance owing is more than $10,000

Payments should be made electronically if you owe more than $10,000. There may be a penalty if you don't pay the amount electronically.

Pay the amount you owe

There are 3 options to pay your balance owing:

  • Online

  • In person

  • By mail

If you cannot pay in full

You can make a partial payment to reduce the amount of interest you need to pay on unpaid amounts.

You can set up a payment arrangement to give yourself more time and flexibility to repay what you owe.

For details, refer to: Arrange to pay your debt over time
