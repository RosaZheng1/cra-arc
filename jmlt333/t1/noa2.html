Personal income tax

Notice of assessments for a tax return (NOA and NOR)

You may be looking for:

  • Get a proof of income statement

A notice of assessment (NOA) is an evaluation of your personal income tax return that the CRA sends you every year after you file your return.

A notice of reassessment (NOR) is an evaluation of changes made to your personal income tax return that the CRA sends you after you file changes to your return.

Receiving your notice

  1. When you will get your notice

    How you file your tax return will affect when you get your NOA.

    As long as you send your tax return on or before the filing due date, we will process it and typically send you your NOA within the following timelines:

    • If you filed online

      • Processing time: Within 2 weeks

      You may get an express notice immediately after filing.

    • If you filed by paper

      • Processing time: Within 8 weeks

    Your NOA may take longer to process:

    If your tax return is a complex tax situation

    Some tax filing situations are more complex and will require additional verification.

    Complex tax filing situations include:

    • Multiple tax returns
    • An international or non-resident, or emigrant return
    • A bankruptcy return
    • A deceased taxpayer return
    • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents
    • Processing time: Approximately within 16 weeks
    If your tax return is selected for a detailed review

    In some situations, your return may be selected for a more detailed review.

    Refer to: Review of your tax return

Accessing a notice for someone else

To access an NOA online for a family member or friend, you must do all of the following:

  • Have them register for My Account
    • Refer to: My Account
  • Be registered in Represent a Client
    • Get your RepID
    • Refer to: Represent a Client
  • Have them authorize you
    • Give your RepID so they can authorize you as their representative
    • Refer to: Representative authorization

Once you are authorized, you will be able to access their NOA.

By mail

If you prefer to have your NOA mailed to you, [or if you don't have My Account?], you need to call the CRA to make the request.

Before you call

  • your social insurance number
  • your full name
  • your date of birth
  • your complete address
  • an assessed return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or to be signed in to My Account

Understanding your notice

  1. Notice details

    1. Personal information

      Text

    2. Access code

      Entering the access code is optional, but recommended.

      Tax software can still send your return even if you don't provide the access code.

      Where to find your access code

      Your 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your Notice of Assessment for the previous tax year.

      If you are filing a tax return for the first time ever, an access code is not needed.

      What happens if you don't provide your access code

      While this access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter your access code:

      • You will not be able to use any information from your 2023 tax return when confirming your identity with the CRA
      • You will have to rely on other information for authentication purposes

  2. Account summary

    This section shows you the result of your assessed or reassessed tax return.

    • A refund

      Refer to: Getting your personal income tax refund

    • A balance owing

      Refer to: Paying the balance owing on your personal income tax

    • No refund or balance owing

    The amount includes any outstanding balances you may owe from previous returns. The account summary may also show the result from concurrent evaluations for multiple year tax returns.

    Why your NOA may indicate you have a balance owing after you paid

    If your NOA indicates you have a balance owing but you already paid your amount, it may be because your NOA was issued before your payment was received.

    Tax returns that are filed online are generally assessed so quickly that any payments sent with a return may not show up on an NOA. Sign into your CRA secure portal to confirm any payments made to the CRA: My Account for individuals

    If you think your amount is incorrect

    If you think your amount is incorrect, review the information in the "Tax assessment" portion of your notice.

    If you find any errors, refer to: Changing your tax return

  3. Tax assessment

    1. Summary

      This section lists the main lines on your assessed tax return. Next to each line are the amounts we used to calculate your refund or balance owing.

      Compare these amounts to the ones on your return to see where we made changes, if any.

      This section also shows any penalty and interest we calculated on your refund or balance owing. If you have a balance owing from a previous assessment, it will also appear here.

    2. Explanation of changes and other important information

      This section explains in detail the changes or corrections we made to your tax return.

      These changes are based on the information sent with your return and the information we have on file.

    3. RRSP deduction limit and available contribution room statement

      This section shows your deduction limit and available contribution room for your Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP).

      Deduction limit

      Your deduction limit is the amount of RRSP contributions you can deduct for the next year.

      Your statement also shows how we calculate your deduction limit, which is based on information you sent us with your previous tax return and information we have on file.

      Available contribution room

      The last line of the statement gives your available contribution room. This is the maximum amount you can contribute for next year.

      It is your deduction limit minus any unused RRSP contributions you reported in past years. Your unused contributions appear on your statement.

      If the total RRSP contributions (current and unused) that you claim on your return are less than your deduction limit, you have available contribution room.

      Excess contribution

      If your RRSP contributions are more than your deduction limit, you have an excess of contributions. You may have to pay tax on this amount.

      For more information on RRSP contribution and deduction rules, refer to: How contributions affect your RRSP deduction limit

    Depending on your situation, your notice may also contain your:

    Home Buyers' Plan (HBP) statement

    If you participate in the Home Buyers’ Plan (HBP), you will see your HBP statement.

    It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

    For more information, refer to: Home Buyers' Plan

    Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP) statement

    If you participate in the Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP), you will see your LLP statement.

    It shows your remaining balance to repay, and your minimum required repayment for the next year.

    For more information, refer to: Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP)

    If you disagree with your assessment

    If you disagree with your assessment and want to register a formal dispute, you have 90 days from the date of your notice to register.

    For more information, refer to: File an income tax objection

No refund or balance owing

If your notice indicates you have no refund or balance owing, it may be because:

  • You paid your balance owing when you filed your return, before your NOA was issued

  • The balance on your return is $0

This means you don't owe the CRA and the CRA doesn't owe you.

Concurrent evaluations for multiple year tax returns

If you file several consecutive-year returns at the same time, we will do a concurrent assessment. For example, if you file your 2023, 2022, and 2021 returns together.

If you send us new information that changes your returns for several consecutive years, we will do a concurrent reassessment.
