Doing and sending your personal taxes

The Digital File My Return service is no longer available. To file your tax return, select one of the options listed below.

You have several ways to do your taxes, including options that are free or have varying costs, or others where you are invited by the CRA.

  • Electronically

    • Typically processed in 2 weeks
    • Fast
    • Easy
    • Secure
    • Automated

  • By paper

    • Takes up to 8 week to process
    • Involves math
    • Uses paper

  • By invitation

    • Typically processed in 2 weeks
    • Only if invited by the CRA

    • Have someone else do your taxes for you

      Depending on which third-party prepares your return, there may be a service fee.

      You can:

      • Authorize a representative to prepare your tax return. This is often:
        • A friend
        • A family member
        • An accountant or bookkeeper
      • Use the services of a discounter
      • Use the services of a free tax clinic

    Within the tax sofware, your tax slips and other personal information can be securely imported from the CRA using Auto-fill my return.

  2. Fill in your tax return

    [intro text]

    1. Enter your personal information

      Enter in your personal information, such as your name, address, and Social Insurance Number (SIN).

    2. Enter your CRA NETFILE access code

      Entering the access code is optional, but recommended.

      Tax software can still elecrontically transmit your return using the CRA's secure NETFILE service, even if you don't provide the access code.

      What is the impact to you if you do not provide the access code

      While this access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter your access code:

      • You will not be able to use any information from your 2023 tax return when confirming your identity with the CRA
      • You will have to rely on other information for authentication purposes
      Where you can find your access code

      Your 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your Notice of Assessment for the previous tax year.

      If you are filing a tax return for the first time ever, an access code is not needed.

    3. Report your income

      What to report as income, including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, or other earnings.

      If you are missing tax slips, refer to: Get a copy of your slips

    4. Claim deductions, credits, and expenses

      Find and claim tax deductions and credits, and get information on tax instalments and expenses.

    [details here]

    • If you used tax software

      1. Pay any service fees to the service provider

        [content here]

      2. Send the digital version of the return to the CRA 

        [content here]

        For details, refer to: NETFILE

        When you must export to a PDF and mail it

        You will have to print and mail your return to the CRA when:

        • You are a non-resident
        • [Your digital tax filing was rejected by the CRA}
      3. Receive a confirmation code from the CRA

        [content here]

    • If you used a professional service provider or tax preparer

      They would have been pre-approved to use a CRA service called EFILE to complete and file your tax return electronically.

      For details, refer to: EFILE for individuals

  4. Pay what you owe or wait for your refund

    There are 3 possible outcomes.

    • Pay what you owe

      Find options to pay now, pay a debt over time or options if you cannot pay.

    • Get a refund

      Check the status of your refund, when to expect it, or how to transfer it to an instalment account.

    • Have no refund and no amount owing

      You don't owe the CRA and the CRA doesn't owe you.

  5. Wait for your tax return to be processed

    Now that you have sent your return to the CRA, you will get a notice of assessment after it is processed.

    After your notice of assessment is issued, you can:

    • Take note of your new NETFILE access code, which can be used for electronic filing during the next tax year
    • Make a change to your tax return, if needed
    • Get a proof of income statement, which may be needed if you apply for a loan

    For details, refer to: After you file your tax return

  2. Fill in your tax return

    [intro text]

    1. Enter your personal information

      Enter in your current personal information (name, address, SIN etc).

    2. Report your income

      What to report as income, including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, or other earnings.

    3. Claim deductions, credits, and expenses

      Find and claim tax deductions and credits, and get information on tax instalments and expenses.

  3. Send your tax return to the CRA

    • Takes up to 8 week to process

    If there are multiple returns being filed:

    • Place each person's return in a separate envelope
    • Place the same person's returns all in one envelope

    • Mailing to the CRA

      1. Complete your taxes on a paper return
      2. Find out where to mail a paper return
      3. Mail your completed tax return to your tax centre

    • Dropping off at a CRA office

      1. Complete your taxes on a paper return
      2. Find out the location of CRA's drop boxes
      3. Place your return in a CRA drop box
