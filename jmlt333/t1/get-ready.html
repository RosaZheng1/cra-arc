Doing and sending your personal taxes

The Digital File My Return service is no longer available. To file your tax return, select one of the options listed below.

You have several ways to do your taxes, including options that are free or have varying costs, or others where you are invited by the CRA.

  2. Fill in your tax return

    [Know what to report and claim]

    Electronically

    1. Enter your personal information

      Enter in your personal information, such as your name, address, and Social Insurance Number (SIN).

    2. Enter your NETFILE access code

      This is optional but recommended.

      Certified tax software and tax applications use the CRA's secure NETFILE service to electronically send your completed income tax and benefit return to us.

      Where to find your access code

      Your 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your Notice of Assessment for the previous tax year.

      If you are filing a tax return for the first time ever, an access code is not needed.

      What if you don't provide the access code

      While this Access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter your Access code:

      • You will not be able to use any information from your 2023 tax return when confirming your identity with the CRA
      • You will have to rely on other information for authentication purposes

    3. Report your income

      What to report as income, including self-employment income, foreign investment income, income from assets, or other earnings.

      If you are missing tax slips, refer to: Get a copy of your slips

      Save time and avoid errors

      Your tax slips and other information can be securely imported from the CRA. Within seconds, parts of your tax return can be auto-filled for you.

      To use Auto-fill my return, you must:

      • Have a CRA My Account
      • Use certified tax software that includes the Auto-fill my return option

      For details, refer to: Auto-fill my return

  4. Pay what you owe or wait for your refund

    There are 3 possible outcomes.

    • Get a refund

      Check the status of your refund, when to expect it, or how to transfer it to an instalment account.

    • Pay what you owe

      Find options to pay now, pay a debt over time or options if you cannot pay.

  5. Wait for your tax return to be processed

    Now that you have sent your return to the CRA, you will get a notice of assessment after it is processed.

Deemed resident

You are a deemed resident of Canada for income tax purposes if:

  • on December 31, 2019, you were living outside Canada, you are not considered to be a factual resident of Canada because you did not have significant residential ties in Canada, and you are a government employee, a member of the Canadian Forces including their overseas school staff, or working under a Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA) assistance program. This can also apply to the family members of an individual who is in one of these situations
  • you stayed in Canada for 183 days or more in the tax year, do not have significant residential ties with Canada, and are not considered a resident of another country under the terms of a tax treaty between Canada and that country
