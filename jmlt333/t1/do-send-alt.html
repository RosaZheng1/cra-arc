Doing and sending your personal taxes

The Digital File My Return service is no longer available. To file your tax return, select one of the options listed below.

You have several ways to do your taxes, including options that are free or have varying costs, or others where you are invited by the CRA.

    • Have someone else do it for you

      Depending on which third-party prepares your return, there may be a service fee.

      You can:

      • Authorize a representative to prepare your tax return. This could be:

        • A trusted friend
        • A trusted family member
        • An accountant
      • Use the services of a discounter
      • Use the services of a free tax clinic (only for those with a modest income and a simple tax return)
    Paper filing

    Using a paper return will require you to:

    • Do several mathmatical calculations manually
    • Print out forms yourself or call the CRA and wait for them to be mailed to you
    • Wait up to 8 weeks for your return to be processed by the CRA

    To get the necessary paperwork, refer to: Get a T1 income tax package

    Invitation-only filing

    • If you received an invite to use SimpleFile

      Individuals with a modest income and a simple return may receive an invite from CRA to participate in SimpleFile.

      The SimpleFile service allows eligible individuals to file their 2023 income tax and benefit return either by phone, digitally or by paper. These simplified methods of filing are free, secure, and easy to use. There are no complex forms to fill out or calculations to do. If you are eligible, you will receive an invitation letter from the CRA to use SimpleFile to file your 2023 income tax and benefit return.

      For full details, refer to: SimpleFile services

    • If you received an invite to call a CRA agent

      Complete and file your taxes over the phone with a CRA agent. The CRA will send an invitation to those eligible through mail or My Account.

      For full details, refer to: Tax filing assistance from a CRA agent

  3. Sending your tax return

    [How you send your return depends on how you choose to do your taxes]

    Electronic filing

    [details here]

    • If you used certified software

      Ideally get your NETFILE Access Code

      For the 2023 tax year, prior to filing your tax return electronically with NETFILE, you will be asked to enter an Access code after your name, date of birth, and social insurance number.

      Where you can find your access code

      Your eight-character Access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your Notice of Assessment for the previous tax year.

      What if you don't provide your access code

      While this Access code is not mandatory, if you do not enter your Access code, you will not be able to use any information from your 2023 tax return when confirming your identity with the Canada Revenue Agency. You will have to rely on other information for authentication purposes. This code does not apply to you if you are filing your tax return for the first time.
