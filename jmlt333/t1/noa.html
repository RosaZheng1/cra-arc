Getting your notice of assessment (NOA)

Your notice of assessment (NOA) is an evaluation of your personal income tax return that the CRA sends you every year after you file your return.

Steps

  1. Know how long it takes

    How you file your tax return will affect when you get your NOA.

    As long as you send your tax return on or before the filing due date, we will process it and typically send you your NOA within the following timelines:

    • Online filing

      • Processing time: Within 2 weeks
      Express NOA

      You may get .....

      For more information, refer to: Express NOA

    • Paper filing

      • Processing time: Within 8 weeks

    Your NOA may take longer to process:

    If your tax return is a complex tax situation

    Some tax filing situations are more complex and will require additional verification.

    Complex tax filing situations include:

    • Multiple tax returns
    • An international or non-resident, or emigrant return
    • A bankruptcy return
    • A deceased taxpayer return
    • A request from the CRA to you or your authorized representative for more information or documents
    • Processing time: Approximately within 16 weeks
    If your tax return is selected for a detailed review

    In some situations, your return may be selected for a more detailed review.

    Refer to: Review of your tax return

  3. Understand your NOA

    Your NOA includes details such as the date we processed your tax return, and how much you may owe or get as a refund.

    1. Review the sections of your NOA

      Your notice contains your:

      Online filing access code (NETFILE)

      If you previously filed online, you can get your online filing access code from your NOA for filing your taxes online again the next year.

      Your 8-character access code is made up of numbers and letters and is located on the right side of your notice of assessment for the previous tax year.

      If you did not file online the previous year, your NOA will not contain an access code.

    2. Confirm your refund or balance owing

      Your NOA will confirm if you have:

      • A refund

        Refer to: Getting your personal income tax refund

      • A balance owing

        Refer to: Paying the balance owing on your personal income tax

      • No refund or balance owing

        You don't owe the CRA and the CRA doesn't owe you.

      Why your NOA may say no refund or balance owing

      If your notice says no refund or balance owing, it may be because:

      • The balance on your return is $0

      • You paid the balance owing when your filed your return, before your NOA was issued

      If you think your amount is incorrect, review the information on your notice of assessment. If you find any errors, refer to: How to change your return
