Payments of stipends, fees or remuneration related to tenure of office (elected or appointed officials)

Content to be covered: Director's fees, Changes to the Non-accountable allowances paid to elected members of legislative assemblies, certain municipal officers, and members of public or separate school boards and Elected or appointed officials

You may be looking for:

  • Payments of fees for services

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement or to the CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you pay stipends, fees (for example, director's or trustee's fees) or remuneration to a person to provide a service in an employment relationship or tenure of office, you need to fill out a T4 slip. This includes payments made to a non-resident elected or appointed official for services rendered in Canada.

Common examples of elected or appointed officials
  • Directors
  • Mayors
  • Municipal councillors
  • School commissioners
  • Chiefs of Indian bands
  • Band councillors
  • Executors
  • Liquidators
  • Administrators of estates
  • Members of a board of referee at the Commission of Canada Employment Insurance
  • Any other position when a person is elected or appointed to that office
When are executor, administrator and power of attorney's fees considered fees for services

Executor fees

An executor is an individual or trust institution named in a will and confirmed by a court to settle a deceased's estate. Generally in situations where an individual dies without a will, or the will does not name an executor, a court will appoint an administrator to handle the deceased's estate. Often, executors and administrators are paid a fee for their services.

Power of attorney fees

When someone has been delegated as a power of attorney, they may be paid a fee for their services. The deductions for the fees paid are treated the same as for executor fees.

Deductions and reporting

Generally, fees paid to an executor, administrator, or a person who has a delegated power of attorney are either considered business income or income from employment or tenure of office.

  • Business income

    Fees earned by a taxpayer for acting as an executor or administrator in the course of business forms part of their business income. The payment is considered fees for services.

    Do not continue. Refer to: Payments of fees for services

  • Income from office or employment

    Fees that are not earned by a taxpayer for acting as an executor, administrator, or a person who has a delegated power of attorney in the regular course of business are considered income from office or employment.

Steps

  1. Determine if the payment made is for a service or for the provision of goods

    • If you make a payment related to provision of goods, this is not a payment for services. Do not continue to next step.
    • If you make a payment related to provision of services, this is considered a payment for services. Continue to Step 2 - Determine if you need to report the payment on a slip.

  2. Determine if you need to report the payment on a T4 slip

    If you make payments for services, you will need to report the payment on a T4 slip if all of the following apply:

    • The payment was made to one of your employee or to a person in tenure of office
    • The individual is elected by popular vote or appointed
    • The individual is entitled to a fixed or ascertainable stipend or remuneration

    The payment is considered as employment income when the payment is received.

    What if you do not know if a worker is an employee or is self-employed

    If you or an individual working for you is not sure of the worker's employment status (employee or self-employed), either one of you can request a CPP/EI ruling to determine the status and whether the employment or the earnings are pensionable, insurable or both.

    Learn more:

    • How to get a CPP/EI ruling
    • Guide RC4110 - Employee or Self-employed?
    • CPP/EI Explained
    • If you do not need to report the payment on a slip, do not continue to next step.
    • If you need to report the payment on a slip, continue to Step 3 - Withhold and report the payment on a slip.

  3. Withhold and report the payment on a slip

    If the payment is considered employment income, you must withhold in the pay period they were received or enjoyed, the following deductions and report on a T4 slip depending whether the amount is paid with or without a salary:

    Most Canadian resident recipients for services performed inside or outside Canada

    Withhold:

    • Payment made without the salary:

      • Withhold CPP, prorated over the number of times the payments are made during the year
      • Do not withhold CPP for a chief of an Indian band or band councillors, except if the employer has filed a CPT124 election
      • Do not withhold EI
      • Withhold income tax only if the payment will exceed the TD1 claim amount

    • Payment made with the salary:

      • Withhold CPP, calculated on both the salary and the payment
      • Do not withhold CPP for a chief of an Indian band or band councillors, except if the employer has filed a CPT124 election
      • Withhold EI on the salary portion only
      • Withhold income tax calculated on both the salary and the payment

    Learn more: How to calculate the deductions

    Report:

    Report the payment on a T4 slip in box 14 - Employment Income (may include other income)

    Learn more: How to report on a T4 slip - Information for employers

    Non-resident recipients for services provided in Canada

    Withhold:

    • Payment made without the salary:

      • Withhold CPP if all the work is performed in Canada (prorated over the number of times the payments are made during the year)
      • Do not CPP if all or part of the work is performed outside Canada
      • Do not withhold EI
      • Withhold income tax only if the payment will exceed the TD1 claim amount
      • Do not withhold income tax for payments related to attending a meeting from outside Canada through electronic means (for example, a teleconference)

    • Payment made with the salary:

      • Withhold CPP calculated on both the salary and the payment
      • Do not CPP if all or part of the work is performed outside Canada
      • Withhold EI on the salary portion only
      • Withhold income tax calculated on both the salary and on the portion related to the services performed in Canada
