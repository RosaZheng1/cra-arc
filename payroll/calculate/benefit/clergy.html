Housing and utilities - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order

You may be looking for:

  • Travel expenses - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you provide a benefit or an allowance  for housing or utilities to your employee in connection with or in the course of their employment duties, the benefit or allowance is taxable. This includes situations where you provide your employee, including the superintendent of an apartment block, with a house, apartment or similar accommodation rent-free or for less than the fair market value (FMV) of the accommodation.

Depending on your situation, your employee who is a member of the clergy may be able to claim a deduction from their income for their residence when filing a personal income tax return.

Steps

  1. Determine if the benefit or allowance you provide to your employee is considered to be for housing and utilities or board and lodging

    You need to determine if the benefit or allowance you provide to your employee is considered to be for housing and utilities or board and lodging. For example:

    • If the benefit or allowance is board and lodging, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more: Board and lodging

    • If the benefit or allowance is housing and utilities, continue to Step 2 - Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance.

  2. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance

    The benefit or allowance for housing or utilities you provide to your employee may not be taxable if the benefit or allowance is in one of the following situations:

    • Your employee is working at a remote location
    • Your employee works at a special work site, including a work site located in a prescribed zone

    • If the benefit or allowance is in one of the above situations, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more on the above exceptions using the links.

    • If the benefit or allowance is not in one of the above situations, continue to Step 3 - Determine if the benefit or allowance is taxable.

  3. Determine if the benefit or allowance is taxable

    Generally, if you provide an allowance  or a reimbursement  for housing and utilities  to your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order, the benefit is taxable. Depending on your situation, the benefit may not be taxable under the Income Tax Act.

    Taxable situation

    The benefit is taxable if you provide or pay on behalf of your employee the following:

    • Free or subsidized board and lodging
    • Allowance or you reimburse for housing or utilities
    Eligibility for a reduction to the amount taxable

    The value of the housing benefit may be eligible for a reduction to the amount taxable if the benefit meets all conditions.

    Other benefits

    If you provide an allowance or you reimburse your employee who is a member of the clergy or a religious order for other benefits, the same tax treatment that apply to most employees also apply them.

    • If the benefit is not related to board, lodging, housing or utilities, do not continue to next step.

      Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable.

    • If the benefit is for board, lodging, housing or utilities, continue to: Step 3 - Determine if your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or a religous order.

  4. Determine if your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or a religous order

    Your employee meets the status and the function conditions if both of the following apply:

    • Your employee's status is one of the following:
      • Member of the clergy
      • Member of a religious order
      • Regular minister of a religious denomination
      Status definitions
      Member of the Clergy
      A "member of the clergy" is a person set apart from the other members of the church or denomination as a spiritual leader. It is not necessary that the process of appointment be referred to as ordination or that the appointment is by someone higher up in the ecclesiastical hierarchy; it may be done by the congregation itself. It is sufficient that there be a formal or legitimate act of recognition, and it requires a serious and long-term commitment to the ministry. Priests, pastors, ministers, rabbis, imams, commended workers and other persons who have been commended, licensed, commissioned or otherwise formally or legitimately recognized for religious leadership within their religious organization may be members of the clergy.
      Religious Order
      The term "religious order" means a group of people bound by the same religious, moral and social regulations and discipline. A religious order may comprise all of the members of an organization or only an identifiable group within that organization. A religious order will have an ascertainable faith with tenets and principles and a requirement for their adherence, and its members will be involved in carrying out the religious mission or ministry of the order. This may include evangelism, religious missions, religious education, social outreach, counseling or relief and development. Not every religious organization is a religious order. Whether or not an organization or a group of individuals within an organization is a religious order is a question of fact to be determined on a case-by-case basis.
      Regular Minister

      A "regular minister" is a person who:

      • is authorized or empowered to perform spiritual duties, conduct religious services, administer sacraments and carry out similar religious functions. Religious functions may include participation in the conduct of religious services, the administration of some of the rituals, ordinances or sacraments, and pastoral responsibilities to specific segments of the religious organization;
      • is appointed or recognized by a body or person with the legitimate authority to appoint or ordain ministers on behalf of or within the denomination; and
      • is in a position or appointment of some permanence.
    • Your employee's function is one of the following:
      • Ministering to a diocese, parish or congregation
      • Engaged exclusively in full-time administrative service by appointment of a religious order or religious denomination

    If your employee meets both conditions, they may be eligible to claim a clergy residence deduction when calculating the value of the benefit and the employment income.

    Learn more: IT141R- Clergy Residence Deduction

    • If your employee does not meet both conditions, your employee is not considered a member of the clergy or a religious order. Do not continue to next step.

      Refer to the information for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable.

    • If your employee meets both conditions, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if your employee is eligible for the clergy residence deduction.

  5. Determine if your employee is eligible for the clergy residence deduction

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 3, your employee is eligible to claim the clergy residence deduction if all of the following apply:

    • The benefit is in respect to rent and utilities paid or the fair market value of the residence and one of the following apply:

      • The benefit is paid on behalf of the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
      • The residence is owned and occupied by the member along with applicable utilities (electricity, heating, water, etc.)
    • Your employee meets the status and function conditions to be considered a member of the clergy or religous order (step 3)
    • The reduction reasonably relate to the degree of disturbance that affects your employee

    Although the clergy residence deduction and the utilities share of the benefit can be excluded from income for the purpose of calculating tax deductions and CPP, you still have to report them on the T4 slip.

    • If your employee is not eligible for the reduction, continue to: Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit.
    • If your employee meets all conditions to be eligible for the reduction, continue to: Step 5 - Get the required documents before you apply the clergy residence reduction.

  6. Get the required documents before you apply the clergy residence reduction

    If your employee meets the conditions in step 4, your employee must provide you with documents before you can apply the reduction.

    Situations

    Situation: You are providing free or low-rent housing (benefits)

    If you are providing free or low-rent housing (benefits), your employee must provide one of the following:

    • A completed Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction, do not send the form to CRA.
    • A letter indicating their member's intention to claim the deduction, including the amount of the deduction that will be claimed.

    You can reduce the income by the amount indicated on line 11 of the Form or the amount indicated on the letter.

    Situation: Your employee owns or rents their residence (allowance)

    If your employee owns or rents their residence (allowance), your employee must do all of the following:

    • Complete Form T1223 Clergy Residence Deduction
    • Complete Form T1213, Request to Reduce Tax Deductions at Source
    • Send Forms T1213 and T1223 to the CRA at the address provided on Form T1213
    • Provide you with the CRA Letter of Authority.

    You can only reduce the income by the amount indicated in the letter.

    Examples

    Example 1: Income tax

    When the clergy member lives in their own house or rents a property and claims a clergy residence deduction based on the fair market value of the house or the rent paid, respectively, they will need to file a T1213, or written letter, with the local tax service office.

    When the request is approved, the employer will reduce the employee's taxable income by the amount of the clergy residence deduction and withhold income tax at source on the difference.

    Example 2: Income tax

    When the clergy member lives in employer-provided accommodations and claims the clergy residence deduction, the deduction will generally be offset by the income inclusion relating to the taxable benefit of employer-provided accommodations.

    In this scenario, there is no change in income for withholding tax purposes, and a T1213 or written request, is not required to be filed.

    When determining the amount of income subject to withholding tax, the employer should include the taxable benefit relating to employer provided accommodations in the employee's income.

    The employee's taxable income should then be reduced by the clergy residence deduction which the employee will be claiming.

    Example 3: CPP

    When calculating the amount of CPP to withhold, the employer should reduce the employee's earnings by the amount of the clergy residence deduction and withhold CPP contributions on the net amount.

    In this situation, the employees must inform their employer, in writing, of their intent to claim the clergy residence deduction (this may include Form T1223).

    If the employee also wants a reduction of income tax at source, as noted above, they must request a waiver by completing and filing Form T1213 with their local tax service office.

    Example 4: CPP

    When calculating pensionable earnings, the clergy residence deduction will generally be offset by the income inclusion relating to the taxable benefit of employer provided accommodations.

    As the deduction and income inclusion generally net out, there are no changes required to the clergy member's income for the purpose of withholding CPP at source.

    In this situation, the employees must inform their employer, in writing, of their intent to claim the clergy residence deduction (this may include Form T1223). However, they need not file a T1213.

    • If your employee does not provide the documents, do not apply the reduction.
    • If your employee provides you with the documents, you can apply the clergy residence reduction.

  7. Calculate the value of the benefit

    The value of the benefit is equal to the fair market value (FMV) of:

    Housing benefit

    • Total value of the housing, board or lodging benefit
    • minus Amount eligible for the clergy reduction amount (step 5)
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and in box 14

    Utilities benefit

    • Total value of the utilities benefit
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and in box 14

    The deduction cannot be more than the amount of the employee's income for the year from the qualifying office or employment.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example 1 - Calculations

    A congregation paid its minister an annual salary of $35,000 and provided a rent free residence valued at $18,000 annually. In addition, the congregation paid for eligible utilities valued at $1,500 annually. The minister will be claiming the clergy residence deduction and has given his employer a letter to that affect.

    • $18,000 is the rent free residence provided by the congregation to be included on the T4 slip using code 30
    • plus $1,500 is the utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40
    • equals $19,500 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using box 14
    • plus $35,000 is the annual salary paid to the minister
    • equals $54,500 is the total amount to be included on the T4 slip using box 14
    Example 2 - Calculations

    A congregation provided a rent-free residence valued at $1,000 per month throughout the year, for a total of $12,000. It also provided a transportation allowance of $425 per month from January through November for a total of $4,675. The clergy member advised the employer that he intends to claim the deduction.

    • $12,000 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 30
    • plus $4,675 is the value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 40
    • equals $16,675 is the total value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using box 14
    Example 3 - Calculations

    A congregation paid its minister a salary together with a monthly cash allowance of $600 for his residence ($7,200 per year). The minister did not advise his employer that he was planning to take the deduction.

    • equals $7,200 is the total value of the benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14

  8. Determine if your employee who is a member of a religious order has taken a vow of perpetual poverty

    You need to determine if your employee decided to take a vow of perpetual poverty. This is a pledge or promise of a person to give their income to the religious order of which they are a member.

    You do not withhold payroll deductions. However, you must report the income on the T4 slip on Box 14 – Employment income, the benefits (if applicable), and both CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt (Box 28).

    Learn more: IT86R- Vow of perpetual poverty

    • If the employee has taken a vow of perpetual property, do not withhold payroll deductions, continue to: Step 8 - Report the benefit on a slip.
    • If the employee has not taken a vow of perpetual property, continue to: Step 7 - Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST.

  9. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

  10. Report the benefit or allowance on a T4 slip

    If the benefit or allowance is taxable, you must report the following on a T4 slip:

    • Most employees - Without a vow of perpetual poverty

      • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction

      • Cash: Option 2

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction

    • Employees with a vow of perpetual poverty (step 6)

      • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 28 - CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction

      • Cash: Option 2

        Report on:

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 28 - CPP/QPP and EI must be checked off as exempt
        • Code 30 - Housing
        • Code 40 - Other information, Utilities or when the clergy member does not claim the clergy residence deduction
