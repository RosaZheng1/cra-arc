Shareholder Benefit Determination Tool

Types of Shareholder Benefits

  • Personal use of company property: If a company asset (e.g., a car, house, or boat) is provided to a shareholder for personal use, this is considered a taxable benefit.
  • Loans to shareholders: Loans provided to shareholders at low or no interest rates can result in a shareholder benefit, calculated as the difference between the interest charged and the prescribed interest rate.
  • Forgiven or low-interest loans: If the company forgives or does not require repayment of loans made to a shareholder, this is considered a taxable benefit.
  • Dividends in lieu of salary: If the shareholder receives dividends instead of salary as part of an arrangement to avoid taxes, it may be deemed a shareholder benefit.
  • Benefits for family members: If the shareholder's family members receive benefits (e.g., use of company assets) that would not be provided to non-shareholding employees, this is also considered a taxable benefit.
  • Personal expenses paid by the company: If the company pays for personal expenses on behalf of the shareholder (e.g., vacations, home renovations), this is a taxable shareholder benefit.
  • Below-market rent: If a shareholder rents property from the company at a rate below the fair market value, the difference is considered a taxable benefit.
