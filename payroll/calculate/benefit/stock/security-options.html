  2. Determine if the security options agreement was made because of an employment relationship

    • If the stock options are not considered received by virtue of employment, they are not taxable as an employee benefit.

      Do not continue to the next step.

    • If the stock options are considered received by virtue of employment, the resulting benefit is generally taxable under section 7 of the ITA.

  3. Determine if the benefit is taxable

    Select the event that caused the employee benefit

    The following events may trigger a taxable benefit:

    Your employee exercises their rights and acquires the shares

    Taxable situation

    The benefit is always taxable to your employee if your employee chooses to exercise their rights under a security option agreement and acquires  the shares under sections 7(1)(a) or 7(1.1) of the ITA.

    • If the benefit is taxable under section 5(1) or 6(1)(a) of the ITA, you must include the benefit in your employee's income in the taxation year in which your employee received the benefit or the cash.

      Continue to: Calculate the total value of the benefit.

    • If the benefit is taxable under section 7 of the ITA, continue to: Determine when the benefit must be included in your employee’s income.

  5. Determine which security options deductions apply to the security options benefit

    Under section 7 of the ITA, your employee may be eligible for options benefit deductions.

    1. Confirm if the stock options agreement is considered cash

      • If the stock options agreement is considered cash, your employee is not eligible for any option benefit deductions.

        Continue to: Calculate the total value of the benefit.

      • If the stock options agreement is not considered cash, your employee may be eligible for options benefit deductions.

        Continue to: Step 5b - Determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under section  110(1)(d) of the ITA.

    2. Determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under section 110(1)(d) of the ITA

      Your employee is eligible to claim the option benefit deduction under 110(1)(d) if all of the following apply:

      • The security is a prescribed share (common shares, per 6204(1) of the employer corporation's capital stock, or those of a non-arm's length corporation) or a unit of a mutual fund trust
      • Your employee dealt at arm's length with the qualifying person (corporation or mutual fund trust) right after the agreement was made
      • At the time when the agreement was made, the price of the share or unit was not less than its FMV 

      Determine if your employee is subject to the $200,000 vesting limit if you are a non-CCPC or mutual fund trust

      If your employee meets all of the above conditions and you are a non-CCPC or mutual fund trust, you need to determine if your employee is subject to the $200,000 vesting limit.

      For security options granted on or after July 1, 2021, your employee is subject to a $200,000 annual vesting limit under 110(1)(d) if the qualifying person meets both of the following conditions:

      • It is not a CCPC
      • Has, or is part of a consolidated group that has, gross revenues of more than $500 million

      • If your employee does not meet all of the above conditions, your employee is not eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d).

        However, they may be eligible for other deductions.

        • CCPC

          If you are a CCPC, continue to: Step 5c - Determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under section 110(1)(d.1) of the ITA.

        • Non-CCPC or mutual fund trust

          If you are a non-CCPC or mutual fund trust, continue to: Step 5d - Determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under section 110(1)(d.01) of the ITA.

      • If your employee meets all of the above conditions, your employee is eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d).

        Continue to: Calculate the total value of the benefit.

    3. If you are a CCPC, determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under section 110(1)(d.1) of the ITA

      Your employee is eligible to claim the option benefit deduction under 110(1)(d.1) if all of the following apply:

      • When the agreement to sell or issue shares to your employee was concluded, the issuing or selling corporation was a CCPC
      • Employee acquired the shares after May 22, 1985
      • Employee dealt at arm's length with the corporation or any other corporation involved right after the agreement was concluded
      • CCPC shares disposed of in the year where your employee dealt at arm's length with the corporation
      • Employee has not disposed of the shares (otherwise than as a result of your employee's death) or exchanged them within two years after the date the taxpayer acquired them
      • Employee did not deduct an amount under 110(1)(d) of the ITA for the benefit

      • If your employee does not meet all of the above conditions, your employee is not eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d.1).

      • If your employee meets all of the above conditions, your employee is eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d.1).

      Continue to: Calculate the total value of the benefit.

    4. If you are a non-CCPC or mutual fund trust, determine if your employee is eligible for the deduction under  110(1)(d.01)

      If your employee acquires a security under an option from a non-CCPC and donates it to a qualified donee  (charitable donations of securities) they are also eligible to claim the option benefit deduction under 110(1)(d.01) if all of the following apply:

      • Donation must be made 30 days from the day the option is exercised
      • Within the year in which the option is exercised
        • Employee must be eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d) of the ITA
        • If the security is a share, it must be of a class listed on a prescribed Canadian or foreign stock exchange

      Under 110(2.1), a deduction under 110(1)(d.01) is available where an employee, in exercising an option, directs the appointed or approved broker or dealer to immediately sell the security and donate all or part of the proceeds of the disposition to a qualified charity. The amount of the deduction must be prorated to reflect the portion of the proceeds that are donated.

      • If your employee does not meet all of the above conditions, your employee is not eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d.01).

      • If your employee meets all of the above conditions, your employee is eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d.01).

      Continue to: Calculate the total value of the benefit.

  7. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    If the benefit is taxable, you must withhold the following deductions:

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      • CCPC

        • Income tax (do not withhold)
        • CPP (do not withhold)
        • EI (do not withhold)
        • GST/HST (do not remit)

      • Non-CCPC or mutual fund trust

        • Income tax
        • CPP
        • EI (do not withhold)
        • GST/HST (do not remit)

    • Cash: Option 2

      • Income tax
      • CPP
      • EI
      • GST/HST (do not remit)

    • Code 86 - Security options election
      Employee is eligible for the deduction under 110(1)(d) and employer can decide if they will deduct the cash-out

      The employer must decide if they will deduct the cash-out as an expense or if your employee will be allowed to claim the stock option deduction.

      Either the employer or your employee can claim a deduction, not both.

      If the employer chooses not to claim the cash-out as an expense, the employer must make an election to do so under 110(1.1). This is done by entering the total amount of the security option cash-out that the employer elected not to claim as an expense using code 86, "Security options election" in the "Other information" of the T4 slip. Your employee can claim the deduction on their T1 return (under paragraph 110(1)(d)).

      The amount the employer reports using code 86 may be different from the taxable benefit they have to include in your employee's income in box 14 and using code 38.
