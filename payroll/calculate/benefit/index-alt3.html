Determine if a benefit is taxable

Before you calculate, determine if a benefit is taxable and if you need to deduct CPP, EI and income tax.

Overview: What is a taxable benefit

Types of benefits

  • Automobile or motor vehicle benefits – Allowances or reimbursements provided to an employee for the use of their own vehicle
  • Automobile provided by the employer
  • Benefits provided to an employee who has a severe or prolonged impairment (disability)
  • Board and lodging
  • Board and lodging, and transportation - Provided to an employee working at a remote location
  • Board and lodging and other benefits provided in special work sites and in prescribed zones
  • Cell phone and internet services
  • Child care expenses
  • Contributions to savings and pension plans

    Includes:

    • First home savings accounts (FHSA)
    • Pooled registered pension plan (PRPP)
    • Retirement compensation arrangement (RCA)
    • Registered disability savings plan (RDSP)
    • Registered education savings plan (RESP)
    • Registered pension plan (RPP)
    • Registered retirement income funds (RRIF)
    • Registered retirement savings plan (RRSP)
    • Tax-free savings account (TFSA)
  • Counselling services and tax preparation
  • Educational assistance

    Includes:

    • Scholarship or bursary, training course or educational program provided to your employee
    • Free tuition or reduced tuition fees, educational allowance, free or subsidized school services provided to your employee's family members
  • Employee security (stock) options
  • Event tickets
  • Gifts, awards, and long-service awards
  • Housing and utilities
  • Housing and utilities - Provided to a member of the clergy or of a religious order
  • Loans and employee debt
  • Loyalty or other points programs
  • Meal provided by the employer

    Includes:

    • Free meals
    • Overtime meals
    • Subsidized meals
    • Meals as part of a collective agreement
  • Medical expenses
  • Merchandise discounts and commissions from personal purchases
  • Motor vehicle provided by the employer
  • Moving and relocation expenses, including housing loss
  • Parking
  • Premiums and contributions to insurance plans

    Includes:

    • Premiums for a group life insurance plan
    • Premiums or contributions to a provincial or territorial hospital, medical care insurance plan or a plan of the Government of Canada
    • Premiums or contributions to non-group insurance plans
    • Premiums or contributions to group sickness or accident insurance plans
    • Contributions to a private health services plan (PHSP)
  • Professional membership dues
  • Recreational facilities and club dues
  • Social events and hospitality functions
  • Tool reimbursements, allowances and rental payments
  • Transportation and airline passes
  • Travel expenses

    Includes:

    • Employee works part-time
    • Employee is an agent selling property or negotiating contracts, or a member of the clergy
    • Employee is a member of the Canadian Forces
    • Employee is travelling away from the municipality and metropolitan area
    • Employee is travelling within the municipality and metropolitan area
    • Employee is sent on a business trip
    • Employee’s spouse or common-law partner on a business trip taken by employee
  • Uniforms, protective clothing, safety and special clothing
