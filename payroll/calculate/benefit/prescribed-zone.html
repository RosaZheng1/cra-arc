Housing and utilities, and travel assistance - Provided to an employee working in a prescribed zone

Content has been updated for clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the current CRA administrative policy.

Generally, if you provide a benefit or an allowance for board and lodging, housing, utilities, transportation or travel assistance to your employee in connection with or in the course of their employment duties, the benefit or allowance is taxable. This includes situations where you provide the benefit to your employee at a cost that is less than the fair market value (FMV) of the benefit.

Depending on your situation, the benefit or allowance may not be taxable for board and lodging, housing, utilities, transportation or travel assistance to your employee who works at a special work site, including in a prescribed zone.

Steps

  1. Determine if any exceptions apply to the benefit or allowance

    The board and lodging, housing, utilities, transportation or travel assistance benefit or allowance you provide to your employee may not be taxable if the benefit or allowance is provided in one of the following situations:

    • Your employee is working at a remote location
    • Your employee is a member of a religious order who has taken a vow of poverty

    It is possible for an employee to work at a location that is both a special work site and a remote work location. However, the benefit can only be excluded from the employee’s income once.

    • If the benefit or allowance is in one of the above situations, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more on the above exceptions using the links.

    • If the benefit or allowance is not in one of the above situations, continue to Step 2 - Determine if the work location is considered a special work site.

  2. Determine if the work location is considered a special work site

    Generally, a special work site is an area where temporary duties are performed by your employee who keeps a self-contained domestic establishment (SCDE) at another location as their principal place of residence.

    What CRA generally considers to be a special work site

    The CRA considers a location to be a special work site based on the following factors:

    1. Determine if the duties performed by your employee are considered temporary

      Generally, the CRA considers that your employee’s duties are temporary (performed on a short-term or interim basis) if they are unlikely to provide continuous employment for your employee for more than 2 years.

      To determine if the work is considered temporary, the CRA considers the following factors:

      • The nature of the duties your employee performs.
      • The total estimated duration of a project or the specific portion of the project for which your employee is hired.
      • The agreed period of time stated in the employment contract or other terms of the employment agreement.

      This must be based on a thorough review of the facts in each specific situation.

      • If the duties performed are not considered temporary, do not continue to the next step.

      • If the duties performed are considered temporary, continue to: Step 2b - Determine if your employee maintained a SCDE at their principal place of residence.

    2. Determine if your employee maintained a SCDE at their principal place of residence

      You must determine if your employee maintained, at another location, a SCDE  as their principal place of residence.

      • If your employee does not maintain a SCDE at another location, do not continue to the next step.

      • If your employee maintained a SCDE at another location, continue to: Step 2c - Determine if the distance between your employee’s SCDE and the work location is not considered reasonable for your employee to return daily.

    3. Determine if the distance between your employee’s SCDE and the work location is not considered reasonable for your employee to return daily

      Generally, the CRA considers that it is not reasonable for your employee to travel between their SCDE  and the work location if the distance between those 2 places by the most direct route normally taken is at least 80 kilometres.

      Even if the distance is less than 80 kilometres, the CRA may still consider that it is not reasonable for your employee to make that trip after taking into account the following factors:

      • The number of hours of work required of your employee at the special work site each shift
      • The time your employee would have for rest if they returned home daily
      • The means of transportation available and condition of the route that would be travelled (for example: the quality of roadway, seasonal weather conditions)
      • Your employee’s general physical and mental health
      • The time it takes to travel the distance, and the time of day the travel takes place
      Special situation - Fishers and seafarers

      A ship may qualify as a special work site when it is at sea for a period of 36 hours or more. Time at sea may include time spent on board because of:

      • Quarantine
      • Waiting for docking
      • Unloading cargo
      • Immigration or customs clearance

      Your employee may qualify for exemption from tax on the value of, or a reasonable allowance for, board and lodging received while on a ship. If all conditions are met, a ship will qualify as a special work site at any time the employee is on board, whether it is in port or at sea.

      • If your employee can reasonably be expected to travel daily between a SCDE and the work location, do not continue to the next step.

      • If the distance travelled is not considered reasonable, the work location meets all conditions in steps 2a, 2b and 2c to be considered a special work site.

    • If the work location is not considered a special work site, do not continue to the next step.

      Learn more about benefits for most employees: Determine if a benefit is taxable.

    • If the work location is considered a special work site (steps 2a, 2b and 2c), continue to: Step 3 - Determine if the special work site is located in a prescribed zone and if it contains a developed rental market.

  3. Determine if the special work site is located in a prescribed zone and if it contains a developed rental market

    1. Determine if the special work site is located in a prescribed zone

      Use the following link to determine if the special work site is located in a prescribed zone: Places located in prescribed zones.

      • If the special work site is not located in a prescribed zone and the benefit or allowance is for housing or utilities , do not continue to the next step.

        Learn more: Housing and utilities

      • If the special work site is a prescribed zone and the benefit or allowance is for housing or utilities, continue to: Step 3b - Determine if the housing or utilities you provided to your employee working in a prescribed zone was provided in a city with a developed rental market.

    2. Determine if the housing or utilities you provided to your employee working in a prescribed zone was provided in a city with a developed rental market

      If you provide housing or utilities to your employee who works in a prescribed zone (step 3a), you must determine if the city where you provide the benefit has a developed rental market to determine the value of the benefit. The following cities in prescribed zones have a developed rental market:

      • Dawson Creek, British Columbia
      • Fort McMurray, Alberta
      • Fort St. John, British Columbia
      • Grande Prairie, Alberta
      • Labrador City, Newfoundland
      • Thompson, Manitoba
      • Yellowknife, Northwest Territories
      • Walbush, Newfoundland
      • Whitehorse, Yukon

      • If the housing or utilities are provided in a city that does not have a developed rental market, when you have to calculate the value of the housing or utilities benefit, you have to consider the ceiling amount  of the benefit.

      • If the special work site is located in a prescribed zone that has a developed rental market, you need to calculate the value of the benefit or allowance using the FMV  of the rent or utility.

    • If the special work site is in a prescribed zone and the benefit or allowance is for board and lodging, transportation or travel assistance, continue to: Step 4 - Determine if the benefit or allowance for board and lodging, transportation and travel assistance must be included in your employee’s income.

    • If the special work site is in a prescribed zone and the benefit or allowance is for housing or utilities, continue to: Step 5 - Determine if the benefit or allowance for housing and utilities is taxable and if your employee is eligible for a reduction.

  4. Determine if the benefit or allowance for board and lodging, transportation and travel assistance must be included in your employee’s income

    Generally, if you provide a benefit or an allowance for board and lodging, transportation or travel assistance to your employee, you need to include the value or the benefit or allowance in your employee’s income.

    How to determine if the benefit or allowance is board and lodging or housing and utilities

    You need to determine if the benefit or allowance you provide to your employee is considered to be for board and lodging or housing and utilities. For example:

    Depending on the situation, if the benefit or allowance is provided to your employee working at a special work site, the amount may be excluded from your employee’s income under the Income Tax Act (ITA).

    Board and lodging

    Under the ITA, if you provide free or subsidized board and lodging or an allowance (not more than a reasonable amount) for board and lodging to your employee who works at a special work site (step 2), including a special work site in a prescribed zone (step 3), the benefit or allowance can be excluded from their income if all of the following apply:

    • Your employee’s duties required them to be away from their principal place of residence or to be at the special work site.
    • Your employee had to work at a special work site where the duties performed were of a temporary nature (step 2a).
    • Your employee kept, at another location, a SCDE  as their place of residence (step 2b) and all of the following apply:
      • Throughout the period, the SCDE was available for the employee’s occupancy and your employee did not rent the SCDE to any other person.
      • Your employee could not reasonably be expected to return daily from the special work site because of distance (step 2c).
    • The benefit or allowance (not more than a reasonable amount) for board and lodging you provided to your employee had to be for a period of at least 36 hours (including time spent to travel between your employee’s principal place of residence and the special work site).

    • If the benefit or allowance for board and lodging is excluded from your employee’s income, you do not need to do any calculations.

      You must report the excluded benefit or allowance related to work sites within 30 kilometres from the nearest urban area with a population of at least 40,000 persons using code 31 on a T4 or using code 124 on a T4A slip. Your employee will use this amount to claim the Northern Residents deduction on their income tax and benefit return.

      You and your employee must fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site.

      Continue to: Step 8 - Report the benefit or allowance on a slip.

    • If the benefit or allowance for board and lodging must be included in your employee’s income, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

      If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA, only the value over that is more than a reasonable amount for board and lodging, transportation or travel assistance must be included in the employee’s income.

      Continue to: Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

    Transportation

    Under the ITA, if you provide free or subsidized transportation or an allowance (not more than a reasonable amount) for transportation to your employee who works at a special work site (step 2), including a special work site in a prescribed zone (step 3), the benefit or allowance can be excluded from their income if all of the following apply:

    • The transportation was provided in a period during which you (or a third party) provided your employee with board and lodging or a reasonable allowance for board and lodging.
    • Your employee’s duties required them to be away from their principal place of residence or be at the special work site for a period of at least 36 hours.
    • The transportation must be between the special work site and your employee’s principal place of residence (including time spent to travel between your employee’s principal place of residence and the special work site).

    • If the benefit or allowance for transportation is excluded from your employee’s income, you do not need to do any calculations.

      You must report the excluded benefit or allowance related to work sites within 30 kilometres from the nearest urban area with a population of at least 40,000 persons using code 31 on a T4 or using code 124 on a T4A slip. Your employee will use this amount to claim the Northern Residents deduction on their income tax and benefit return.

      You and your employee must fill out Form TD4 Declaration of Exemption - Employment at a Special Work Site.

      Continue to: Step 8 - Report the benefit or allowance on a slip.

    • If the benefit or allowance for transportation must be included in your employee’s income, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

      If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA, only the value over that is more than a reasonable amount for transportation must be included in the employee’s income.

      Continue to: Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

    Travel assistance

    If you provide a benefit or an allowance for travel assistance to your employee who works at a special work site (step 2) in a prescribed zone (step 3), the benefit or allowance is not taxable in the following situations:

    • The travel assistance is for:

      • Medical travel assistance
      • It was paid or reimbursed under the terms of a private health services plan
    • The travel assistance was provided for business travel.

    • If the benefit or allowance for travel assistance is not taxable, you do not need to do any calculations.

    • If the benefit or allowance for travel assistance must be included in your employee’s income, the benefit or allowance is taxable.

      You must report the total amount of taxable travel assistance using code 32 on a T4 slip (or using code 028 on a T4A slip) and if any part of the taxable travel assistance was for medical travel, you also need to report the taxable medical travel assistance using code 33 on a T4 slip (or using code 116 on a T4A slip).

      If you do not report the amount of the taxable travel assistance which was for medical travel, the CRA will consider all of the travel assistance to be for vacation or other travel and your employee will not be entitled to claim a deduction for medical travel.

      Depending on the situation, continue to:

      • Step 5 - Determine if the benefit or allowance for housing and utilities is taxable and if your employee is eligible for a reduction.
      • Step 6 - Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance.

  5. Determine if the benefit or allowance for housing and utilities is taxable and if your employee is eligible for a reduction

    1. Determine if the benefit or allowance for housing and utilities is taxable

      Continue to: Step 5b - Determine which method you can use to calculate the housing or utilities benefit.

    2. Determine which method you can use to calculate the housing or utilities benefit

      You need to calculate the value of the benefit depending if the prescribed zone has a developed rental market or not (step 3b).

      • Developed rental market

        Where the prescribed zone has a developed rental market, use the FMV  of the benefit you provided for housing or utilities.

      • Without developed rental market

        Where the prescribed zone does not have a developed rental market, you need to compare the FMV  of the benefit you provided with the ceiling amount  to determine the value. The value of the housing or utilities benefit will be the lesser of the FMV and the ceiling amount.

        The ceiling amount for housing or utilities benefits is the maximum amount you can include in an employee’s income if the employee does not pay any expenses related to housing or utilities.

        Monthly ceiling for housing and utilities

      Continue to: Step 5c - Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit.

    3. Determine if your employee is eligible for a reduction to the housing benefit

      1. Suitability of size - Accommodation larger than needed
      2. Loss of privacy and quiet enjoyment

  6. Calculate the value of the benefit or allowance

    If the benefit or allowance is taxable, you must calculate the value of the benefit based on the following benefit provided:

    Board and lodging
    • FMV  of the board and lodging that is taxable
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit for board and lodging to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA (step 3), only the value over a reasonable amount for board and lodging must be included in the employee's income.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example - Calculation

    Your company provided board and lodging to an employee working at a special work site. The FMV of the board and lodging provided during the tax year was $2,500. Your employee reimbursed the company $500 for the benefit. The board and lodging provided must be included in the employee’s income because the benefit did not meet the conditions to exclude it from their income as the employee did not keep a SCDE at another location and their duties were not of a temporary nature.

    • $2,500 is the FMV of the board and lodging that is taxable
    • minus $500 is the amount your employee reimbursed you
    • equals $2,000 is the value of the benefit for board and lodging to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
    Transportation
    • FMV  of the transportation that is taxable
    • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
    • equals Value of the benefit for transportation to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14 (or code 028 on a T4A slip if you are not their employer)

    If the allowance is more than a reasonable amount but meets all other conditions under the ITA, only the value over that is more than a reasonable amount for transportation must be included in the employee's income.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Example - Calculations

    Pierre was employed by a company to work at a special work site. Pierre was provided transportation between the special work site and their principal place of residence by the employer. The value of the transportation provided to Pierre is $2,250. The transportation must be included in his income as the transportation was not provided in a period where board and lodging were also provided and he was only required to be at the special work site for 24 hours. He had reimbursed the employer $250 for this benefit.

    • $2,250 is the FMV of the transportation provided to Pierre
    • minus $250 is the amount Pierre reimbursed his employer
    • equals $2,000 is the value of the benefit for transportation to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14 of Pierre’s T4 slip
    Travel assistance

    • Special work site not in a prescribed zone

      • Value of the travel assistance
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for travel assistance to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer paid their employee working at a special work site which was not in a prescribed zone a flat allowance to subsidize the cost of airfare for personal recreational trips taken by the employee. The total amount paid by the employer was $5,600, none of which was related to employment-related duties.

      • $5,600 is the value of the travel assistance
      • minus $0 as the employee did not reimburse the employer
      • equals $5,600 is the value of the travel assistance benefit to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14

    • Special work site in a prescribed zone

      • Value of the taxable travel assistance in a prescribed zone
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of benefit for taxable travel assistance in a prescribed zone to be included on the employee’s T4 slip using code 32 (and code 33 if part of the travel assistance is related to medical travel) and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer provided their employee working in a prescribed zone with plane tickets for two distinct trips over the course of the tax year. One plane ticket, which had a FMV of $2,000, was for the employee to take a trip from the special work site to a nearby city to undergo a medical procedure which was not available in the area where they were working. The other plane ticket was provided by the employer to help the employee go on a family vacation. The FMV of the ticket was $850.

      • $2,850 ($2,000 + $850) is the total value of the taxable travel assistance
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $2,850 is the value of benefit for taxable travel assistance in a prescribed zone to be included on the employee’s T4 slip using code 32 and box 14, and $2,000 is the value of the benefit for taxable medical travel assistance to be included using code 33
    Housing or utilities

    • Special work site not in a prescribed zone with a developed rental market

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • FMV  of the housing that is taxable
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Calculate the utilities benefit
      • FMV  of the utilities that is taxable
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer provided their employee with housing which has a fair market value of $4,000 in a prescribed zone with a developed rental market. The accommodations were owned by the employer.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $4,000 is the FMV of the housing that is taxable
      • minus $0 as the employee did not reimburse the employer
      • equals $4,000 is the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    • Special work site in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market - Housing you own

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • Lesser of the FMV  of the housing that is taxable and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Calculate the utilities benefit
      • Lesser of the FMV  of the utilities that is taxable and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer provided their employee with housing with a fair market value of $4,000 in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market . The accommodations were owned by the employer. The ceiling value of the housing was $3,000.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $3,000 is the value of the housing that is taxable because the ceiling amount was less than the FMV
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $3,000 the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    • Special work site in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market - Housing you rent from a third party

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • Lesser of the amount you pay the third party for the housing that is taxable and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14
      Calculate the utilities benefit
      • Lesser of the amount you pay the third party for the utilities that is taxable and the ceiling amount 
      • minus Any amounts your employee reimbursed you
      • equals Value of the benefit for utilities to be included on the T4 slip using code 40 and box 14
      Example - Calculation

      An employer paid a third party to provide their employee with housing with a fair market value of $4,000 in a prescribed zone without a developed rental market. The accommodations were owned by the third party. The ceiling value of the housing was $3,000.

      Calculate the housing benefit
      • $3,000 is the value of the housing that is taxable because the ceiling amount was less than the amount you pay the third party
      • minus $0 because the employee does not reimburse the employer
      • equals $3,000 is the value of the benefit for housing to be included on the T4 slip using code 30 and box 14

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

  7. Withhold payroll deductions and remit GST/HST

    The withholding and remitting requirement depends on the type of remuneration: cash , non-cash , or near-cash .

    You must withhold the following deductions:

    • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, exception for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone
      • CPP
      • EI (do not withhold)

      Remit:

      • GST/HST in certain situations

    • Cash: Option 2

      Withhold:

      • Income tax, exception for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone
      • CPP
      • EI

      Do not remit:

      • GST/HST (do not remit)
    Exception: Income tax withholding for payments for travel assistance in a prescribed zone

    For travel assistance payments in a prescribed zone (step 3), where your employee agrees in writing to use the payment entirely for vacation or medical travel, you may waive the requirement to withhold income tax depending on where they live:

    • If your employee lives in a prescribed northern zone, you may waive the full amount of the payment.
    • If your employee lives in a prescribed intermediate zone, you may waive 50% of the payment.

    Learn more: Places located in prescribed zones.

    The amounts must be included in the pay period they were received or enjoyed.

    Learn how to calculate deductions and the GST/HST to remit: How to calculate - Calculate payroll deductions and contributions

  8. Report the benefit or allowance on a slip

    If the benefit or allowance is taxable, you must report the following on a T4 or T4A slip:

    • T4 slip - Employer

      • Non-cash and near-cash: Option 1

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Board and lodging (taxable board and lodging, transportation, and housing)
        • Code 31 - Special work site (excluded board and lodging, and transportation in a prescribed zone)
        • Code 32 - Travel assistance in a prescribed zone (total taxable amount including medical travel you must include in their income)
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (only medical travel you must include in their income)
        • Code 40 - Other taxable benefits (including travel assistance outside a prescribed zone and transportation you must include in their income)

      • Cash: Option 2

        • Box 14 - Employment income
        • Box 24 - EI insurable earnings
        • Box 26 - CPP/QPP pensionable earnings
        • Code 30 - Board and lodging (taxable board and lodging, transportation, and housing)
        • Code 31 - Special work site (excluded board and lodging, and transportation in a prescribed zone)
        • Code 32 - Travel assistance in a prescribed zone (total taxable amount including medical travel you must include in their income)
        • Code 33 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (only medical travel you must include in their income)
        • Code 40 - Other taxable benefits (including travel assistance outside a prescribed zone and transportation you must include in their income)

    • T4A slip - Payer

      • Code 028 - Other income (total taxable amount including medical travel you must include in their income)
      • Code 116 - Medical travel assistance in a prescribed zone (only medical travel you must include in their income)
      • Code 124 - Special work site (excluded board and lodging, and transportation in a prescribed zone)
