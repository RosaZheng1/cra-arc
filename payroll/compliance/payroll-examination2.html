Payroll assisted compliance review - Checklist

1. Review of employee records

  • Complete employee files: Verify that all employee files contain necessary documentation (for example: contracts, performance reviews).
  • Accurate information: Ensure employee details are accurate, including name, address, and SIN.
  • Onboarding documents: Confirm that onboarding documents are complete and signed (for example: tax forms, policies).

2. Payroll accuracy

  • Payroll reports: Review payroll reports for accuracy and completeness.
  • Deductions verification: Confirm that all payroll deductions (CPP, EI, income tax) are correctly applied.
  • Overtime calculations: Ensure overtime is calculated according to the applicable laws and policies.

3. Compliance with employment standards

  • Wage compliance: Check that all employees are paid at least the minimum wage as per provincial regulations.
  • Hours of work: Verify compliance with maximum hours of work and mandatory rest periods.
  • Vacation entitlements: Ensure vacation entitlements are provided according to employment standards.

4. Tax reporting and remittances

  • Timely remittances: Confirm that payroll tax remittances are made on time to CRA.
  • T4 and T4A slips: Ensure T4/T4A slips are accurately completed and distributed to employees.
  • Annual summary compliance: Review annual summaries for discrepancies or errors.

5. Benefits administration

  • Benefit plan documentation: Ensure documentation for all employee benefits (health, dental, retirement) is available.
  • Eligibility verification: Confirm employee eligibility for benefits based on company policies.
  • Reporting of taxable benefits: Verify that all taxable benefits are reported correctly on T4 slips.

6. Occupational health and safety compliance

  • Health and safety policies: Review policies to ensure compliance with workplace health and safety regulations.
  • Training records: Confirm that all employees have completed required health and safety training.
  • Incident reporting: Ensure proper procedures are in place for reporting and investigating workplace incidents.

7. Employee communication and training

  • Policy communication: Verify that all employees are aware of workplace policies and procedures.
  • Training programs: Review training programs to ensure they meet compliance requirements and are up-to-date.
  • Feedback mechanisms: Ensure there are channels for employees to provide feedback on compliance issues.

8. Record retention policies

  • Retention schedule: Confirm that records are kept according to the company’s record retention schedule.
  • Secure storage: Ensure that employee records are stored securely to protect privacy.
  • Destruction of records: Verify proper procedures are in place for the destruction of outdated records.

9. Audit and monitoring procedures

  • Regular audits: Confirm that regular internal audits are conducted to assess compliance.
  • Issue resolution: Ensure a process is in place for addressing compliance issues identified during audits.
  • Monitoring changes in legislation: Keep up-to-date with changes in employment and tax legislation that may impact compliance.

10. CRA correspondence and follow-Up

  • Documentation of correspondence: Maintain records of all correspondence with CRA.
  • Response to audits: Ensure timely responses to any CRA audits or inquiries.
  • Action plan for compliance issues: Develop an action plan for any compliance issues identified in CRA correspondence.
GST/HST examination review - Checklist

1. GST/HST registration

  • Registration verification: Confirm that the business is registered for GST/HST, if applicable.
  • Collecting GST/HST: Ensure that GST/HST is being collected on taxable supplies.
  • Exempt supplies: Identify any exempt supplies and verify proper handling in the accounting records.

2. Input Tax Credits (ITCs)

  • Claiming ITCs: Verify that ITCs are claimed for eligible purchases.
  • Supporting documentation: Ensure that proper documentation (invoices, receipts) is maintained for all claimed ITCs.
  • Proportional ITCs: Confirm that any prorated ITCs are calculated correctly for partially exempt supplies.

3. GST/HST returns

  • Filing frequency: Check that GST/HST returns are filed on time according to the assigned frequency (monthly, quarterly, annually).
  • Accurate reporting: Ensure that reported amounts match the supporting documentation and accounting records.
  • Payment of net tax: Confirm that any net tax owing is paid by the due date to avoid penalties.

4. Record keeping

  • Retention of records: Verify compliance with the CRA's record retention requirements for GST/HST records.
  • Organized documentation: Ensure that records are organized and easily accessible for review.
  • Electronic records: Confirm that electronic records are backed up and secure.

5. Audit preparedness

  • Preparation for Audits: Review procedures to ensure readiness for a potential GST/HST audit.
  • Response Plan: Develop a plan for responding to audit inquiries or information requests from CRA.
  • Internal Review: Conduct regular internal reviews of GST/HST compliance and practices.

6. Staff training

  • Training on GST/HST: Provide training for staff on GST/HST requirements and compliance practices.
  • Updates on changes: Keep staff informed of any changes to GST/HST legislation or policies.
  • Communication channels: Establish clear communication channels for GST/HST-related questions and issues.

7. Compliance monitoring

  • Regular reviews: Conduct regular reviews of GST/HST processes and compliance.
  • Identifying issues: Identify and address any issues related to GST/HST compliance proactively.
  • Tracking changes: Monitor changes in GST/HST legislation that may affect compliance requirements.
Employer compliance audit - Checklist

1. Employee and payroll records

  • Employee information: Verify SINs, addresses, and dates of employment.
  • Employee contracts: Confirm employment contracts are up-to-date and include details on salary, benefits, and stock options.
  • Payroll deductions: Review deductions for CPP, EI, income tax, and other applicable deductions.
  • Payroll register: Ensure that payroll amounts match what’s reported on T4 and T4A forms.

2. Taxable benefits and allowances

  • Documentation of benefits: Confirm documentation for taxable benefits (for example: vehicle, housing, stock options).
  • Valuation of benefits: Verify that benefits are properly valued per CRA guidelines (for example: fair market value for personal use).
  • Allowances: Check if all cash allowances are included in income, such as travel or meals.

3. Statutory and source deductions

  • Remittance schedule: Confirm remittance of payroll deductions on time as per CRA remittance schedule.
  • CPP, EI, and Income Tax: Ensure all mandatory deductions have been made and recorded.
  • Records of employment (ROEs): Review issued ROEs and ensure they match employment termination dates.

4. Tax filings and reporting

  • T4 and T4A slips: Verify issuance of T4/T4A slips for all employees and contractors with proper amounts.
  • T4 and T4A summaries: Ensure T4/T4A summaries match CRA requirements and reconcile with payroll records.
  • Corporate tax filings: Check if the employer’s business income tax filings are complete and filed on time.

5. Stock options and share-based compensation

  • Documentation: Verify plan documentation for stock options and share-based compensation.
  • Taxable benefit calculation: Ensure the taxable benefit is calculated for eligible options.
  • Reporting: Confirm that benefits are reported on T4s where required.

6. Employee expense reimbursements

  • Expense policy compliance: Review the policy for business expense reimbursement (for example: travel, meals).
  • Supporting documentation: Verify receipts and records for all employee reimbursements.
  • Tax treatment: Ensure compliance with CRA rules on taxable vs. non-taxable reimbursements.

7. Pension plans and retirement savings programs

  • Plan documentation: Confirm documentation of any Registered Pension Plans (RPP) and Group RRSPs.
  • Employer contributions: Verify employer contributions are in line with CRA maximums.
  • Reporting: Ensure contributions are correctly reported on employee T4 slips.

8. Health benefits and other non-cash benefits

  • Benefit plan documentation: Confirm eligibility, coverage, and plan documentation.
  • Non-taxable benefits: Verify that non-taxable benefits are in line with CRA guidelines (for example: health benefits).
  • Taxable benefits: Ensure taxable benefits are correctly reported and valued.

9. Employment agreements and termination records

  • Termination agreements: Verify severance packages and termination payouts align with CRA rules.
  • Final payroll calculations: Ensure final pay and benefits are accurately calculated and remitted.
  • Record of employment (ROE): Confirm that ROEs are issued in a timely manner.

10. CRA correspondence and compliance records

  • CRA communication: Keep records of all CRA correspondence regarding audits or inquiries.
  • Adjustments or penalties: Document any CRA-mandated adjustments or penalties and verify corrections.
