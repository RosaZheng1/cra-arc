The payroll and GST/HST examination process

Learn about the process

What is a payroll and GST/HST examination process?

Generally, an examination is simply a routine check to ensure that the information you provided on your information return is correct and you meet all your reporting obligations. An examination is not a tax audit.

How the process works

  1. Account selection - CRA contacts you to inform you that your account has been selected for review
  2. Preparation - CRA contacts you to begin the review process, prepare necessary documents
  3. Examination - CRA reviews your records either in person or as a desk review.
  4. Completion - CRA provides a detailed report on the results of the examination.

What are common topics reviewed

  • Employee income - Includes regular income, bonuses, and more
  • Deductions - Review of CPP, EI, and income tax deductions
  • Employee benefits - Covers taxable benefits such as parking or vehicle allowances
  • Remittances - Ensures timely payments to the CRA
  • GST/HST - Reviews collected GST/HST and input tax credits on GST/HST returns

Final assessment

CRA's detailed findings may include one of these outcomes:

  • No changes required - Your records are compliant
  • Adjustments required - Discrepancies found, adjustments recommended
  • Penalties & interest - May apply for non-compliance

Responding to the CRA

You may provide additional documents or explanations to address any identified discrepancies.

Best practices for employers

  • Keep accurate records - Maintain thorough payroll and GST/HST documentation
  • Remit on time - Timely remittances help avoid penalties
  • Stay compliant - Regularly review CRA updates and payroll compliance rules
