Payroll and examination process

What is a payroll examination and GST/HST account review process?

A CRA-led review of an organization’s payroll and GST/HST books and records to ensure accurate remittances and compliance with Canadian payroll laws.

The steps in the payroll review process

  1. Notice of review: CRA sends a notice informing the employer about the payroll review.
  2. Document request: Employer provides payroll records, T4 summaries, and other documents.
  3. In-person vs desk review: CRA reviews the records in-person or desk review.
  4. Compliance check: CRA examines records for compliance with payroll laws.

Common topics reviewed

Employee income: Regular wages, bonuses
Deductions: CPP, EI, income tax
Employee benefits: Taxable benefits (for example: insurance, vehicle allowances)
Remittances: Timely payments to the CRA
GST/HST collected and input tax credits (ITC) claimed on taxable benefits

Review outcome

No Changes required: Payroll records are compliant.
Adjustments Needed: CRA identifies discrepancies and recommends adjustments.
Penalties/interest: CRA may apply penalties or interest for non-compliance.

Responding to the CRA

The employer can submit additional documents or explanations to clarify any discrepancies found in the review.

Final assessment

CRA provides a final report of the review outcome, detailing any changes or payments required.

Best practices for employers

Keep accurate records: Maintain thorough payroll documentation.
Remit on time: Ensure timely and accurate remittances to avoid penalties.
Stay compliant: Regularly review payroll compliance rules and CRA updates.
