Payroll Examination Review Results

1. Full Compliance

If the CRA finds no issues during the payroll examination, you will receive a report confirming that your payroll records are compliant. No further action is required, and you will not owe any additional taxes or penalties.

2. Minor Discrepancies Found

Small discrepancies may be found, such as minor errors in deductions or reporting. The CRA will ask for corrections to be made, but penalties or interest may be avoided if the errors are corrected promptly.

3. Under-Remittance of Taxes

If the CRA finds that you have under-remitted income taxes, CPP, or EI contributions, you will be required to pay the outstanding amounts. The CRA will provide a notice detailing the amounts owed, and interest may be applied to the overdue payments.

4. Penalties and Interest Imposed

In cases of significant non-compliance, such as repeated errors or late remittances, the CRA may impose penalties along with interest on any overdue taxes or contributions. The CRA will issue a formal notice of the penalties, and employers may be required to make immediate payment.

5. Reclassification of Employees

If the CRA determines that certain workers classified as independent contractors should have been treated as employees, you may be required to retroactively remit employment taxes (CPP, EI) for these individuals. Correcting misclassified workers can lead to significant additional taxes and penalties.

6. Required Follow-Up Audit

If serious issues are found or if there is a history of non-compliance, the CRA may schedule a follow-up audit. This can involve a deeper review of your payroll records and practices to ensure that all corrections have been made and compliance is maintained.

7. Appeal Process

If you disagree with the CRA’s findings, you have the right to appeal. You can file a Notice of Objection to dispute the results. If necessary, the dispute can be escalated to the Tax Court of Canada.
