GST/HST and First Nations peoples

Content has been updated to remove the word Indian where it is not a direct reference to the Indian Act, including improving clarity, completeness and plain language. No changes were made to the existing legislative requirement.

Meaning of the terms “First Nations individual” and “First Nations band”

On this webpage, the term “First Nations individual” is used to describe an individual who is registered or entitled to be registered under the Indian Act. The term “First Nations band” is used to describe the primary levels of Indigenous government (band councils and tribal councils)

We recognize that many First Nations people in Canada prefer not to describe themselves as Indians. However, we sometimes use the term “Indian” because it has a legal meaning in the Indian Act.

Generally, everyone has to pay tax in Canada, except when the customer is a First Nations individual , First Nations band  or Band-empowered entity  that is eligible for GST/HST relief because of section 87 of the Indian Act.

Steps

  1. Determine if the First Nations Goods and Services Tax (FNGST) or the First Nations Tax (FNT) applies

    Some band councils or other governing bodies of First Nations have signed self-government agreements with the Government of Canada that include withdrawal from tax relief under section 87 of the Indian Act.

    This webpage is not for you if you want to find out how the FNGST or the FNT applies. The tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act does not apply to settlement lands.

    If FNGST or the FNT applies, refer to:

    • First Nations Goods and Services Tax
    • First Nations Tax

    If FNGST or FNT does not apply, continue to Step 2 - Confirm if the business is a GST/HST registrant.

  2. Confirm if the business is a GST/HST registrant

    • If the business is a GST/HST registrant, they generally must charge the GST/HST.

      Continue to Step 3 - Get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity.

    • If the business is not a GST/HST registrant, they generally do not charge the GST/HST.

      Do not continue to next step.

    Learn more about who needs to register, or if a business can register voluntarily: When to register for and start charging the GST/HST

  3. Get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity

    The vendor must get proof that the customer is a First Nations individual, First Nations band or band-empowered entity for the tax exemption under section 87 of the Indian Act to apply.

    Learn more from Indigenous Services Canada: About Indian status

    Customer

       Documents the vendor must obtain from the customer
    First Nations individual 

    The vendor must get proof that the individual is registered with Indigenous Services Canada from one of the following:

    • Secure Certificate of Indian Status

Meaning of a remote store

Generally, a remote store refers to a store in one of the following situations:

    • The store is located more than 350 kilometres from the nearest community with a population of 5,000 or more
    • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 50% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities
    • Surface transportation is not available year-round on paved or gravelled roads linking the store to the nearest community
    • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 50% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities
    • The store is within 10 kilometres of a reserve
    • During the previous 12-month period, made more than 90% of its sales to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities

A store in a remote location but that doesn't meet the sales condition because it does not have a sales history may still qualify as a remote store based on a reasonable expectation of such sales. An example of a store without a sales history is a newly opened store.

Vendor that qualifies as a remote store

Vendors who choose to operate as a remote store must keep records of their sales made to First Nations individuals, First Nations bands and band-empowered entities.

If the vendor qualifies and decides to operate as a remote store, each year, they have to let their tax services office know how they are operating.
