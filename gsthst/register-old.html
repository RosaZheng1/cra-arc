Find out if you have to register for a GST/HST account

The interactive tax tool (ITT) gives you personalized results based on your answers to a series of questions. The tool uses plain language to explain the most common tax situations. It is provided as a guideline only and does not replace the law.

The accuracy of your results depends on the accuracy of the information you enter. Later calculations made by the CRA may be different.

This tools does not collect or store the information you enter.

You can only use the tool if your business started receiving revenue in less than 8 calendar quarters or if your business has not started receiving revenue yet.

Use this ITT to:

  1. Find out if you have to register and when to register
  2. When to start charging the GST/HST

Even if you consider yourself to be self-employed, you might be an employee. To determine whether you’re an employee or self-employed, ask yourself the following questions:

You are a taxi operator or a commercial ride-sharing driver

A taxi business means a business carried on in Canada of transporting passengers by taxi for fares that are regulated under the laws of Canada or a province. A driver who supplies taxable commercial ride-sharing services on or after July 1, 2017, is considered to be a taxi business for GST/HST purposes.

Do you know for sure whether you are an employee or self-employed? (required)

You are usually self-employed if you are in one of the following situations:

  • You own your taxicab/motor vehicle
  • You lease a taxicab/motor vehicle from an owner for a flat fee, either on a daily, weekly, or monthly basis
  • You lease a taxicab/motor vehicle from an owner for a percentage of fares

Examples of taxable supplies

Examples of taxable supplies are:

  • Sales of new housing
  • Sales and rentals of commercial real property
  • Sales and leases of automobiles
  • Car repairs
  • Soft drinks, candies, and potato chips
  • Hotel and other short-term accommodation
  • Clothing and footwear
  • Taxi and limousine transportation
  • Legal and accounting services
  • Franchises
  • Barber and hairstylist services

Examples of zero-rated supplies (taxable at 0%)

Some examples of taxable supplies are:

  • Basic groceries such as milk, bread, and vegetables
  • Agricultural products such as grain, raw wool, and died tobacco leaves
  • Most farm livestock
  • Most fishery products such as fish for human consumption
  • Prescription drugs and drug-dispensing services
  • Certain medical devices such as hearing aids and artificial teeth
  • Feminine hygiene products (as of July 1, 2015)
  • Exports (most goods and services for which you charge and collect the GST/HST in Canada, are zero-rated when exported)
  • Legal and accounting services
